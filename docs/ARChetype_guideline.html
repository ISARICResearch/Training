Background

The ISARIC-WHO Clinical Characterisation Protocol (CCP) framework is a global, inclusive initiative offering a comprehensive approach for the rapid and standardised characterisation of (re)emerging infectious diseases. It supports researchers by providing standardised tools, ensuring consistent data collection and high-quality data. ISARIC also offers resources for data management and an automated pipeline for data analysis. Central coordination enhances communication and collaboration among researchers, leveraging a global network of ISARIC collaborators.

Objectives

This document outlines ISARIC’s methodology for developing archetype CCP CRFs, detailing the stages from need identification to release and feedback mechanisms, adaptable to epidemic and disease contexts.

Process

1. Identify the Need

Any ISARIC staff or research partner may propose a new CRF by submitting justification, requirements, and objectives to the ISARIC CCP team.

2. Endorsement of Action

The CCP Coordinator seeks endorsement from the ISARIC Executive Board (EB) if the topic is outside ISARIC’s usual scope, considering public health impact, research gaps, stakeholder engagement, and ISARIC’s unique contribution.

3. Identify the CRF Owner and Approver

The CCP coordinator collaborates with the proposer to designate an Owner (oversight) and Approver (validation). The Approver must be an ISARIC member or partner.

4. Identify Experts Reviewers

In consultation with the EB, relevant stakeholders, including experts, researchers, clinicians, and regulatory bodies, are invited for input, ensuring alignment with diverse requirements.

5. Identify Relevant Literature and Projects

The Owner, supported by the CCP Coordinator, reviews existing literature, clinical trials, and guidelines to inform CRF development.

6. WHO Consultation

The CCP Coordinator liaises with WHO to align the CRF with WHO’s initiatives and involve relevant parties in its development.
